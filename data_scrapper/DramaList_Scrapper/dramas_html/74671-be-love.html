Tamamori is a writer, and Miyata, an editor. Together they are working on a picture book, which is nearing completion. They visit a romantic seaside villa, where their days are filled with love, in order to finish it. But alas - twisted fate deems this happiness to be curtailed. Their love story explores themes of jealousy, marriage, and bereavement. ~~ Based on Kis-My-Ft2's Miyata Toshiya and Tamamori Yuta's songs entitled 'Be Love', 'Hoshi ni Negai wo', and 'Unmei'. Boring and confuse plot

the story from the beginning is slow, very slow, even sleepy. We see the arrival at the house of two boyfriends or a future couple, but not a real couple, since the painter who is a guest at the house that this colleague owns rejects him at the first attempt at a kiss in the house. They tell us that they collaborate in a job to illustrate a book. the scenes of mixing the illustrated book with the real characters are rare and tiresome. then the development of the bromance… there is no passion, that is clear, therefore the issue of couple rings as a flashback is not understood. no couple of just friends does that. The painter has a mental mess with the issue of being a vampire... if he is, why does he go to the beach? he eats like the most... or is it just imagination? ...the weird call about someone's anointing...it's plot nonsense. On the other hand, there is no affective chemistry and a whole mess of time frames that lead us to boredom. Come on, I don't recommend it at all.

Essa série não está entre minhas preferidas, pois eu detesto finais tristes, porém as séries japonesas são excelentes no quesito roteiro e atuação, e isso por si só aguça minha curiosidade. Os atores interpretam eles mesmos e isso também é um ponto interessante.
A fotografia dessa série é belíssima, e a história, por ser real nos deixa muito envolvidos. Mas eu não suporto o luto e a tristeza, apesar da beleza da atuação e fotografia, apesar de saber que uma história real não deve ter seu final modificado (ou não será real), eu continuo tendo uma certa aversão aos finais tristes.
